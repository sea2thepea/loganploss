Logan Ploss is a student at the Crane School of Music, SUNY Potsdam, class 2025, with a major in music business and a concentration in voice. He is a member of the vocal studio of Dr Donald George, tenor, and receives private lessons. As a music business student, Logan also learns to manage the operations of his original band called PINS, (People In Need of Supervision) as they influence the music scene of Potsdam, NY.

Logan began as a guitarist in 2018 when he took lessons from Kenny Hohman, owner of Troy Music Academy and guitarist of the Troy native band Super 400. Through taking lessons at Troy Music Academy, Logan had the chance to have recital performances with other students. Most notably, Logan led the band for the Beatles rooftop concert tribute in 2019.

Logan Ploss started performing on his own after releasing an album of original music on February 1st, 2021 called Century Ride. In tandem with the release, Logan performed original sets at local coffee shops to promote the project. Starting school in the fall of 2021, Logan continued to write and perform new songs at the school's nightclub Hurleys.

Logan formed the band PINS in the fall of 2022, with bassist Jacob Sullivan, and drummer Vatressa Teamoh. The band performs a mixture of covers and original material and has gigged since its inception. PINS has performed a headlining set at Hurleys nightclub, is two time champ of Potsdam's annual battle of the bands competition (Battle of the Bands 2022, Battle of the Bands 2023), and has performed house sets in town to spread their influence beyond the campus.
